1. Project Overview
1.1 Executive Summary
UPI Merchant Category Pulse Dashboard is a data analytics portfolio project that surfaces consumer spending trends from India's payments ecosystem using publicly available NPCI and RBI data. The dashboard tracks which merchant categories are growing faster than the UPI network overall — delivering the kind of macro consumer insight that fintech product, risk, and strategy teams produce internally.
The project is designed to serve two audiences simultaneously: a recruiter or hiring manager evaluating analytical depth, and a business stakeholder needing a clear, actionable view of spending momentum across categories.
1.2 Problem Statement
The Business QuestionUPI crossed 228 billion transactions in 2025 — but aggregate numbers hide the real story. Which merchant categories are outpacing the network? Where is consumer spending shifting? Which categories are declining in average ticket size even as volume grows? This dashboard answers those questions from official government data, with no proprietary access required.
1.3 Project Goals
Primary: Build a live, interactive dashboard deployed on Streamlit Cloud with a public URL
Surface a Momentum Score metric that identifies categories growing faster than UPI overall
Tell a data-driven business story in the README with 4–5 non-obvious findings
Demonstrate end-to-end analytical workflow: ingestion → cleaning → metric design → visualization → deployment
Secondary: Demonstrate business storytelling ability beyond dashboarding
Showcase familiarity with Indian fintech ecosystem — relevant for CCIL, PhonePe, Razorpay, and similar roles
Add a third distinct project archetype to the portfolio (macro/trend analysis, complementing existing churn prediction and SQL analytics work)

3. Metrics & Analytical Design
3.1 The Momentum Score
Core Metric DefinitionMomentum Score = (Category 3-month volume CAGR) ÷ (Overall UPI 3-month volume CAGR)A score > 1.0 means the category is growing faster than the UPI network as a whole. A score < 1.0 means it is lagging. This single number is the hook — it answers the business question directly and is simple enough to explain in 30 seconds in an interview.
3.2 Supporting Metrics
Metric
Formula / Method
Business Meaning
Volume Share %
category_volume / total_volume × 100
Category's share of the UPI pie each month
YoY Growth Rate
(this month – same month last year) / same month last year
Removes seasonal noise for fair comparison
Avg Ticket Trend
Rolling 3-month avg of avg_ticket
Is the category premiumising or commoditising?
Seasonality Index
Month's volume / trailing 12-month avg
Identifies festive spikes (Oct-Nov) vs. off-season dips
Volume Share Shift
Current 3M share – same 3M prior year
Which categories are gaining / losing wallet share

4. Dashboard Design & Features
4.1 Dashboard Sections
Section A — Market Overview
Dual-axis line chart: overall UPI volume (left axis) and value in ₹ crore (right axis), Jan 2022 – latest month
Annotated with key events: COVID recovery, festive seasons (Oct–Nov), RBI policy changes
Summary KPI cards: Total volume (latest month), YoY growth %, avg ticket size, number of active categories
Section B — Category Momentum Leaderboard
Horizontal bar chart of all categories ranked by Momentum Score, colour-coded: green (> 1.5), amber (0.8–1.5), red (< 0.8)
Time window filter: 3-month / 6-month / 12-month trailing window
Toggle: rank by Momentum Score vs. absolute volume vs. YoY growth
Section C — Category Deep Dive
Dropdown to select any category
Four sub-charts: monthly volume trend, monthly value trend, avg ticket size trend, volume share % over time
YoY comparison overlay: current year vs. prior year (same category)
Auto-generated plain-English summary: e.g., 'Travel grew 2.4x faster than UPI in the last 6 months. Avg ticket size is ₹3,200 — the highest across all categories.'
Section D — Insight Cards
4–5 static cards with the non-obvious findings, displayed prominently
Each card: Finding headline + 1-sentence explanation + data source callout
Example: 'Grocery is UPI's volume engine, but it's shrinking per-transaction — avg ticket dropped 22% in 2 years as kirana adoption expands.'

5.3 Known Risks & Mitigations

Likelihood
Mitigation

High — confirmed

6. README & Portfolio Presentation
6.1 README Structure
The README is as important as the code. It is the first thing a hiring manager reads after clicking the GitHub link. It must follow a story structure, not a technical spec.
1. The problem — one paragraph on why aggregate UPI numbers hide the real consumer story
2. Data sources — NPCI and RBI links, brief note on cleaning challenges (category label drift)
3. Methodology — plain-English explanation of Momentum Score with a worked example
4. Key Insights — 4–5 findings, each with a chart screenshot and a one-sentence business implication
5. Business Recommendations — 2–3 actionable suggestions (e.g., 'Travel-adjacent fintechs should prioritise Q4 UPI checkout optimisation')
6. How to run locally — three commands max
7. Live demo link — top of README, not buried at the bottom
6.2 Resume Bullet (Final)
Copy-paste ready for your resume"Built an end-to-end UPI merchant category analytics dashboard on NPCI/RBI public data (2022–2025); engineered a Momentum Score metric that identified Travel and Insurance as the fastest-growing P2M categories, outpacing overall UPI growth by 2.4x — surfacing insights relevant to fintech product and risk teams."
6.3 Interview Talking Points
Interview Question
Talking Point
Walk me through a project.
Lead with the business question. Then: data source, the Momentum Score metric I invented, and the Travel insight. End with the live URL.
What was the hardest part?
NPCI changes category label names across monthly files — 'Food & Beverages' vs. 'F&B' vs. 'Food Delivery'. Building the mapping dictionary and validating totals took most of Week 1.
What would you do next?
Layer Google Trends data for the same categories to see if search intent leads transaction volume by 1–2 months. That would make the dashboard a leading indicator, not just a lagging report.
Why this dataset specifically?
It's official regulatory data, fully public, and directly relevant to how payments and fintech companies think about market sizing. I wanted a project where the data source itself adds credibility.

7. Out of Scope
The following are explicitly excluded from v1.0 to keep the project completable in 2–3 weeks:
ML forecasting model — adding ARIMA or Prophet to predict next-month category volumes. This can be a v2 extension mentioned in the README.
Google Trends enrichment — correlating search volume with UPI transactions. A strong 'what's next' talking point but not in scope.
State-level analysis — RBI does publish state-wise UPI data. Excluded because it requires a separate data pipeline and adds scope without proportional portfolio value.
Real-time data refresh — NPCI data is monthly; the dashboard will update monthly via a manual data refresh, not automated.
User authentication or personalisation — this is a public portfolio project, not a SaaS product.
